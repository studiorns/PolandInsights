Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

60.81% (+4.81pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 60.81%, Poland has moderate awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating significant opportunity for growth.
  • The +4.81pp year-over-year growth suggests positive momentum in awareness building efforts.
  • This moderate awareness level suggests that continued investment in awareness-building campaigns is necessary.
  • The positive trend indicates that current marketing strategies are effective and should be maintained or expanded.
  • With awareness in the moderate range, there is substantial room for growth before reaching market saturation.
  • Marketing should continue to focus on broad reach campaigns while beginning to incorporate more targeted messaging to drive consideration.

Consideration

27.51% (+5.51pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 27.51%, consideration is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • However, the substantial +5.51pp increase shows strong positive momentum that should be leveraged.
  • This suggests that while many Polish travelers know about Abu Dhabi, there are still barriers preventing them from actively considering it as a destination.
  • The significant gap between awareness (60.81%) and consideration (27.51%) indicates an opportunity to focus marketing on addressing specific barriers.
  • The strong positive trend in consideration (+5.51pp) suggests that recent marketing efforts are successfully addressing some barriers.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing the specific concerns identified in market research, particularly authenticity concerns (26%) and perceptions about restrictive laws/customs (24%).

Intent

10.08% (+2.08pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 10.08%, intent is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The +2.08pp increase shows positive momentum, though not as strong as the consideration growth.
  • This suggests that even among those who consider Abu Dhabi, very few have immediate plans to visit.
  • The positive growth in intent (+2.08pp) indicates that tactical marketing efforts are having some success.
  • The low intent level suggests a need for more tactical, conversion-focused campaigns that create urgency and address practical barriers to booking.
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency through limited-time offers, highlighting unique experiences, and addressing practical barriers such as flight availability and visa processes.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.37 (+0.01)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 10.08% ÷ 27.51% = 0.37

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.37, the conversion from consideration to intent is poor, indicating that 37% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The minimal increase of +0.01 suggests that while both consideration and intent are growing, they are growing at similar rates.
  • This poor conversion ratio indicates significant barriers between consideration and actual travel planning.
  • The ratio suggests that once Polish travelers consider Abu Dhabi, there are still substantial barriers preventing them from forming concrete travel plans.
  • Marketing should focus on understanding and addressing the specific barriers that are preventing consideration from converting to intent.
  • The slight improvement in this ratio (+0.01) is positive but minimal, suggesting that more targeted efforts are needed to improve conversion efficiency.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Poland market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

3.0 days

The average number of nights Polish travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 3.0-day average stay indicates a short vacation timeframe, typical of many European markets.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 3-day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend-plus packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 3-day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The relatively short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time.

Booking Window

62 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Polish travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 62-day booking window is relatively long, indicating a market that plans trips well in advance.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 2-3 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for considered decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Early booking incentives are likely to be effective for this market.
  • The long booking window suggests that both digital and traditional media can be effective, as there is sufficient time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This extended planning period also provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements over time.
Strategic Example:

For the August-September peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in June to align with the 62-day booking window. Early booking discounts offered 75+ days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging even earlier commitments.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Aug & Sep

The months with the highest volume of Polish hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Polish guests.

Business Implications:
  • August (4,724 guests) and September (4,685 guests) represent the clear peak season for Polish travelers, with November (2,715) as a strong third.
  • This late summer/early fall peak differs from some other European markets and aligns with Polish holiday periods.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (July, October) to extend the peak periods.
  • Winter months represent a significant growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting indoor activities and special winter offers.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.61 (moderate-high seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Poland travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if January has a travel query index of 11.41 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 7.1, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.61 (11.41 ÷ 7.1).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.61 value indicates that the market has moderate-high seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 61% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 62-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The moderate-high seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.

Media Elasticity

0.40 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Poland market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.40 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 4.0%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive and moderately strong, suggesting good ROI potential.
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI with moderate efficiency.
  • The planned 89.8% increase in media impressions for 2025 should drive approximately a 35.9% increase in travel queries, aligning well with the 35.8% growth forecast.
  • The moderate elasticity suggests that the Poland market is somewhat responsive to media campaigns, but other factors also significantly influence travel decisions.
  • This moderate responsiveness suggests that media investments should be complemented by other marketing approaches, such as addressing specific barriers to visitation like authenticity concerns and perceptions about restrictive laws.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign, you can expect approximately an 8.0% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.40 = 8.0%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.78 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Poland travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.78 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with good confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with greater certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.78) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±7.2%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±7.2%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±7.2% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 139.2 and 160.8 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Poland market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Polish travelers cite for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses from Polish travelers who are aware of Abu Dhabi but do not consider it as a potential travel destination.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Not Authentic Enough: 26% (-2pp vs Q1 2021)
  • Restrictive Laws/Customs: 24% (-4pp vs Q1 2021)
  • Destination Preference: 23% (-6pp vs Q1 2021)
  • Safety Concerns: 22% (+1pp vs Q1 2021)
  • Lack of Activities: 21% (-2pp vs Q1 2021)
Business Implications:
  • Authenticity concerns remain the top barrier despite a 2pp improvement since 2021.
  • Perceptions about restrictive laws and customs have improved significantly (-4pp) but remain a major barrier.
  • Preference for other destinations has seen the largest improvement (-6pp), suggesting Abu Dhabi's competitive position is strengthening.
  • Safety concerns are the only barrier that has increased (+1pp), requiring attention.
  • The perception of "lack of activities" has improved slightly (-2pp) but remains significant.
Strategic Recommendation:

Develop an "Authentic Abu Dhabi" campaign featuring genuine cultural experiences and local traditions to address the top barrier. Create educational content about Abu Dhabi's laws and customs, highlighting recent liberalization and visitor-friendly policies. Implement a "Safe Destination" campaign featuring safety rankings and testimonials to counter the increasing safety concerns.

  • The substantial increase in "Perception as Destination for Older People" (+1pp) indicates a growing image problem among younger Polish travelers.
  • The increase in "Negative Word of Mouth" (+1pp) suggests that reputation management needs attention.
  • The emergence of "Visa Difficulties" (+1pp) indicates practical barriers that may be preventing travel.

Conclusion & Strategic Recommendations

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Poland. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Poland market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Moderate awareness (60.81%) with low consideration (27.51%) and intent (10.08%), indicating significant barriers to conversion
  • A poor intent-to-consideration ratio (0.37) indicating substantial barriers between consideration and actual travel planning
  • A relatively short average stay (3.0 days) indicating short-break vacation patterns
  • A long booking window (62 days) allowing for effective campaign planning
  • Seasonality concentrated in August and September, with strong growth potential in other seasons
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.40) suggesting reasonable response to media investments
  • Strong interest in both cultural attractions and theme parks, with Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque and Ferrari World being the most searched attractions
  • Preference for luxury waterfront accommodations, with Rixos and InterContinental being particularly popular
  • Significant barriers including authenticity concerns (26%), restrictive laws/customs (24%), and destination preference (23%)
  • Improving perceptions across most barriers, with only safety concerns showing a slight increase (+1pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Addressing the significant gap between awareness and consideration through targeted messaging that addresses specific barriers
  • Developing an "Authentic Abu Dhabi" campaign featuring genuine cultural experiences to address the top barrier
  • Creating educational content about Abu Dhabi's laws and customs, highlighting recent liberalization
  • Implementing a "Safe Destination" campaign to counter the increasing safety concerns
  • Highlighting the balance of cultural attractions and entertainment venues to appeal to the diverse interests
  • Developing compact itineraries that maximize the typical 3.0-day stay, focusing on high-impact experiences
  • Timing campaigns to align with the 62-day booking window, particularly for the August-September peak period
  • Creating marketing content that showcases luxury waterfront accommodations alongside cultural attractions
  • Developing partnerships with popular hotel brands like Rixos and InterContinental
  • Creating targeted campaigns for the summer months to balance the seasonal concentration
  • Developing tactical, conversion-focused campaigns to improve the poor intent/consideration ratio

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Poland market.
